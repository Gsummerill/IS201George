Rules of the Game

In pickleball four people/two teams play against eachother until one of the teams reaches 11 points. A point is scored when either one team is unable to return the ball to the other side of the net or the ball bounces twice. Teams can only score points when they are serving the ball. In order to serve the ball, as an opposing team, you must stop both services from the offensive team. Each player when on the offensive side recieves a chance to serve the ball. Starting with the right box on the court the first server puts the ball in play, once the point is lost the second server recieves a turn to serve. After the scoring of each point the offensive teams players switch sides. The only exception to both servers having an opportunity to serve is when the game starts, the first starting offensive team only has one serve.

Pickleball is a fun and friend building sport, treat others with good sportsmanship and you will have a blast! Have fun and study the shots to hit below!

Skills

  1. The Serve
    • When Serving the ball must be hit underhand below the navel.
    • Serve to the diagnolly opposite service court from behind the baseline.
    • The serve must land in the diagnolly opposite box for play to begin, if missed the point is forfeited by the offensive team.
  2. The Return
    • The return is the second shot of the game and is hit by the recieving team.
    • Hit the return back across the net to any of the boxes of the court, aim for the back line to make your return harder play from.
  3. The Dink
    • The purpose of a dink shot is to land the ball in the NVC(No Volley Zone)
    • Hitting effective dinks will keep the ball in play and tough for the opponent to return
